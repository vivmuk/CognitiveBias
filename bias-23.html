Endowment Effect

Overvaluing What We Own

Intermediate decision making Chapter 23

What is it?

We value things more highly simply because we own them. This leads to irrational attachment to possessions and reluctance to make beneficial trades.

Why it matters

Once we own something, we value it more than we did before we owned it, and more than others value it. This makes us reluctant to sell or trade items even when it would be beneficial.

Key Insight

"People who own stocks often hold onto losers too long and sell winners too early."

How Endowment Effect Works

The Mechanism

Once we own something, we value it more than we did before we owned it, and more than others value it. This makes us reluctant to sell or trade items even when it would be beneficial.

How to Detect It

Notice when you're reluctant to sell or trade something you own, even when offered a good deal. Ask yourself if you'd pay the current market price to acquire the item if you didn't already own it.

Countermeasures

  • Regularly reassess your possessions as if you don't own them
  • Ask 'Would I buy this at today's price?' for items you own
  • Set clear criteria for when to sell or replace items
  • Practice small trades to overcome attachment bias

Real-World Example
